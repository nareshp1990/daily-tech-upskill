## 3. Agentic AI & Tooling

**MCP crosses 97 million installs**
Anthropic's Model Context Protocol hit 97 million installs in March 2026, cementing its position as the de-facto standard for tool integration in AI agents. Most major IDE plugins, CI platforms, and SaaS vendors now expose MCP-compatible endpoints.

**Claude Code goes standalone**
Anthropic launched Claude Code as a standalone product — a terminal-native agent that clones repos, writes and runs tests, fixes failing CI pipelines, and opens pull requests autonomously. Now available outside of Claude.ai via direct install.

A new OutSystems / Gartner report finds 96% of enterprises are already using AI agents; Gartner predicts 40% of enterprise apps will include task-specific agents by end of 2026. The flip side: 94% of organisations report concern over AI sprawl driving complexity, technical debt, and security risk.

## 4. Funding & Business

**OpenAI — $122B raised**
OpenAI closed a $122 billion funding round to accelerate "the next phase of AI" — primarily targeting compute infrastructure, safety research, and enterprise expansion. Largest single funding event in tech history.

**DARPA CLARA programme**
DARPA is offering up to $2M per award over 24 months for high-assurance AI projects, with a hard requirement that all produced software be released under Apache 2.0. Proposals closed April 10; awards expected Q3 2026. Signals growing government interest in verifiable, open AI.

Mistral released Large 3 with improved function calling accuracy and structured output reliability, plus EU data residency on La Plateforme — positioning it as the GDPR-compliant alternative for European teams that can't use US-hosted models.

## 5. Research & Breakthroughs

**TurboQuant (Google, ICLR 2026)**
Google Research unveiled TurboQuant at ICLR 2026 — an algorithm that dramatically reduces KV cache memory overhead using PolarQuant vector rotation and Quantized Johnson-Lindenstrauss (QJL) compression. Practical implication: longer context windows at the same GPU cost.

**Explainable AI (XAI) demand surges**
Enterprise adoption of AI agents is driving a parallel surge in demand for XAI systems that can transparently articulate the reasoning behind decisions — particularly in regulated industries (finance, healthcare). Several startups announced XAI-as-a-service offerings this week.

## 6. Regulation & Policy

**US federal vs. state AI governance showdown**
The December 2025 Trump executive order attempting to pre-empt state AI laws is heading toward a judicial confrontation. Multiple states (California, Colorado, Texas) are proceeding with their own AI regulatory frameworks regardless. Legal clarity on which layer governs enterprise AI deployments remains unresolved.

**EU AI Act enforcement ramp-up**
EU regulators confirmed that high-risk AI system audits under the AI Act begin in earnest in Q3 2026. Enterprises using AI in hiring, credit scoring, or critical infrastructure must have conformity assessments ready.
