## 3. Agentic AI — Phase 2 Begins

### Microsoft Agent Governance Toolkit (April 2)
- Open-source (MIT license) **runtime security governance** for autonomous AI agents.
- Addresses the critical gap: agents can now act autonomously, but who governs what they're allowed to do?
- Provides policy enforcement, audit trails, and safety boundaries for production agents.

### The "Phase 2" of Agentic Development
- Industry consensus: Phase 1 (single-agent chat) is over. Phase 2 is **multi-agent systems executing real workflows**.
- Every major model release in 2026 now emphasises agentic capabilities as a first-class feature.
- Open models from Mistral, Meta (Llama 4), and DeepSeek now **match commercial models** on many benchmarks at a fraction of the cost.

## 6. Regulation Watch

### Federal vs. State Tension
- The defining regulatory conflict of 2026: **federal "innovation-first" deregulation** vs. **state-level enforceable AI rules**.
- **Colorado AI Act**: Now active — documentation requirements, transparency mandates, and risk mitigation for high-risk AI systems.
- **California AI Transparency Act**: In effect since Jan 1 — requires disclosure of AI-generated content and training data summaries.

### Public Sentiment
